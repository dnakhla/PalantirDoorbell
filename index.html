Your Right to Privacy in Public Spaces

While this project demonstrates AI surveillance capabilities, it's important to understand the legal framework surrounding privacy in public spaces and residential areas.

In most jurisdictions, there is no expectation of privacy in public spaces visible from public property. However, the use of AI for automated analysis and long-term tracking raises new legal and ethical questions.

This POC operates entirely on your own property, analyzing footage from your own camera, for your own use.

The legal landscape around AI surveillance is rapidly evolving. Several cities and states are implementing restrictions on automated surveillance systems, facial recognition, and predictive policing technologies.

Legal Considerations

Public vs. Private: You can record what's visible from your property, but cannot point cameras at neighbors' private spaces like backyards or windows.
Data Retention: Consider implementing automatic deletion of old footage. Long-term storage of identifiable information may have legal implications.
Commercial Use: Using surveillance data for commercial purposes (like selling insights) may require additional legal considerations and permissions.
Disclosure: Some jurisdictions require disclosure of surveillance systems. Check local laws about signage and notification requirements.
AI Bias: Automated systems can exhibit bias. Consider the ethical implications of AI-powered surveillance and pattern recognition.
This information is for educational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice. Consult with a legal professional before implementing any surveillance system. Laws vary significantly by jurisdiction and are constantly evolving.
